For player 1:

\[ u_2(x_1, x_2) = \begin{cases} -x_2, & \text{if } x_2 \leq x_1 \\ v_2 - x_1, & \text{if } x_2 > x_1 \end{cases} \]

A Nash equilibrium in this game will satisfy the condition that neither player can increase their payoff by unilaterally changing their own stopping time, given the stopping time of the other player.

Let's consider the best responses for each player.

- If \( x_1 < x_2 \), player 1's best response is to increase \( x_1 \) to be equal to \( x_2 \), as this would increase their payoff from negative to \( v_1 - x_2 \).
